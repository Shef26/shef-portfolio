About Me

Engineer by training.
Product manager by conviction.

I didn't start in product — I started by writing Java APIs for trade-finance systems at Wipro, working directly on-site with banking clients. It was an incredible experience: being in the room with MDs and Directors, translating complex business requirements into systems that actually worked, and seeing how decisions made at a whiteboard turned into real consequences for real operations. That kind of exposure — early, high-stakes, cross-functional — shaped how I think about product work to this day.

That perspective shapes how I approach product now. I'm less interested in shipping features for the sake of it, and more focused on building things that actually get used — and make sense to the people using them.

After engineering, I moved into product more intentionally through my Master's at Duke University, where I used every project and experience to get closer to real product problems. Since then, I've found myself drawn to complex, slightly chaotic spaces — where user needs, business goals, and technical constraints don't neatly align.

Currently, I'm working on the report builder tool for enterprise software at Aurigo, and exploring how natural language can make data more accessible for customers.

At the core, I care about two things: deeply understanding what customers actually need (not just what they say), and giving teams enough clarity to build the right thing — without overcomplicating it.
